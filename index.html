Adhan — Phrase by Phrase

Each phrase carries its own emotional weight. Understand what you're saying and your delivery will carry that truth naturally.

ٱللَّٰهُ أَكْبَرُ
Allāhu Akbar
Allah is the Greatest
× 4
Opening — sets the tone. The moment of command. Let the first takbir land with full chest resonance before the second.
أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا ٱللَّٰهُ
Ashhadu an lā ilāha illā Allāh
I bear witness that there is no god but Allah
× 2
Declaration of Tawhid — emotion first peaks here. Soften into it. The word "illā" is your pivot point.
أَشْهَدُ أَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا رَسُولُ ٱللَّٰهِ
Ashhadu anna Muḥammadan rasūlu Allāh
I bear witness that Muhammad is the Messenger of Allah
× 2
Warm, reverent, intimate. Many reciters drop volume slightly here to convey deep love.
حَيَّ عَلَى ٱلصَّلَاةِ
Ḥayya ʿalā ṣ-ṣalāh
Come to prayer
× 2
The call to action — your voice rises and opens. This is where Saba inflections shine. Turn right on the first, left on the second.
حَيَّ عَلَى ٱلْفَلَاحِ
Ḥayya ʿalā l-falāḥ
Come to success
× 2
Climactic phrase — the deepest emotional resonance. Let "falāḥ" hang in the air. This is the moment the community feels the call.
ٱللَّٰهُ أَكْبَرُ
Allāhu Akbar
Allah is the Greatest
× 2
Return — grounding after the peak. Come back down gently. The congregation is ready.
لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا ٱللَّٰهُ
Lā ilāha illā Allāh
There is no god but Allah
× 1
Closing — gentle resolve. Let silence do the final work. The Adhan ends in stillness.

Maqamat — Musical Modes

Understanding maqamat lets you recite with intention rather than imitation. Your voice — deep with upper range — can access all four beautifully.

Hijaz
حجاز
SudaisShamsan
Majestic, commanding, desert vastness
The most iconic maqam for Adhan. Built on a distinctive augmented second interval that gives it that haunting, elevated quality. When people close their eyes and imagine the Adhan, they're hearing Hijaz.
For Your VoiceDeep voices own this maqam. You're in your natural lane — lean into it.
Bayati
بياتي
AbkarBukhatir
Warm, intimate, deeply human
The most used maqam in Arabic music and recitation. Natural, conversational quality. Many reciters default to Bayati because it feels like home — accessible, forgiving, and deeply emotional without being heavy.
For Your VoiceWorks across all ranges. Great for long prayers — doesn't fatigue the congregation.
Rast
راست
Bukhatir
Balanced, grounded, clear
Think of Rast as the foundation. Stable, resolved, and pure. Many imams use Rast as their base for prayer recitation because it doesn't fatigue voice or congregation during long Tarawih nights.
For Your VoiceIdeal for Tarawih and long salah. Sustainable, not taxing — use as your resting mode.
Saba
صبا
Shamsan
Deeply sorrowful, longing, spiritually piercing
Saba is the maqam of tears. It carries a quality of spiritual yearning that is almost impossible to fake. Shamsan's genius is knowing exactly when to invoke it — usually at the Hayya phrases — and when to pull back.
For Your VoicePowerful for deeper voices. Use sparingly at peak moments — overuse dilutes its power.
